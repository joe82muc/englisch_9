📘 Present Perfect – How it works

▼
have / has + past participle (3rd form)  →  help → helped  ·  write → written  ·  be → been
TypeExampleDeutsch
✅ PositiveI have just tidied my room.Ich habe gerade mein Zimmer aufgeräumt.
❌ NegativeI haven't been to South Africa yet.Ich war noch nicht in Südafrika.
❓ QuestionHave you ever met a famous person?Hast du jemals eine berühmte Person getroffen?
⏱ for / sinceI haven't seen Mia for a week.Ich habe Mia seit einer Woche nicht gesehen.

⏱ for vs. since

FOR = Zeitspanne
for an hour for two years for a long time for a week
SINCE = Zeitpunkt
since Sunday since 6:00 a.m. since 1994 since I was young

🔑 Signal words

already just not … yet never ever (questions) since for

Level A – Fill in the gaps. Use the Present Perfect (have/has + past participle). The verb is given in brackets. Use 💡 for grammar tips, 🤖 for AI hints without the answer!
Exercise 1
I to South Africa a few times.  (already / be)
📌 Formel: Subjekt + have/has + already + Partizip
Das Subjekt ist I → du brauchst have (nicht has).
Das Verb ist be → unregelmäßig: be – was – been
✏️ Muster: I have already been …
Exercise 2
There serious problems with AIDS for many years.  (be)
📌 Formel: There + have/has + been + …
Problems ist Plural → du brauchst have (nicht has).
Das Verb ist be → unregelmäßig: be – was – been
✏️ Muster: There have been serious problems …
Exercise 3
Mia in South Africa since she was young.  (live)
📌 Formel: Subjekt + has + Partizip (bei he/she/it)
Das Subjekt ist Mia = she → 3. Person Singular → has
Das Verb ist live → regelmäßig: live + d = lived
✏️ Muster: Mia has lived in South Africa …
Exercise 4
South African people a lot since 1994.  (achieve)
📌 Formel: Subjekt + have + Partizip (bei Plural)
South African people = Plural → du brauchst have
Das Verb ist achieve → regelmäßig: achieve + d = achieved
✏️ Muster: … people have achieved a lot since 1994.
Exercise 5
you ever to South African music?  (ever / listen)
📌 Frage-Formel: Have/Has + Subjekt + ever + Partizip?
Das Subjekt ist you → du brauchst Have am Satzanfang.
Das Verb ist listen → regelmäßig: listen + ed = listened
✏️ Muster: Have you ever listened to … ?
Exercise 6
I the zoo for two years.  (not visit)
📌 Verneinungs-Formel: Subjekt + haven't/hasn't + Partizip
Das Subjekt ist I → du brauchst haven't (= have not)
Das Verb ist visit → regelmäßig: visit + ed = visited
✏️ Muster: I haven't visited the zoo …
Exercise 7
The star in South Africa.  (already / sing)
📌 Formel: Subjekt + has + already + Partizip
Das Subjekt ist the star = he/she → 3. Person Singular → has
Das Verb ist sing → unregelmäßig: sing – sang – sung
✏️ Muster: The star has already sung in South Africa.

Level B – Choose for or since. Remember: for = Zeitspanne (duration) · since = Zeitpunkt (starting point). Brauchst du Hilfe? Nutze 💡 oder 🤖!
Exercise 1
I haven't seen Mia ________ Sunday.
📌 Sunday = ein bestimmter Tag = Startpunkt oder Zeitspanne?
Exercise 2
I haven't seen Mia ________ a week.
📌 a week = wie lang? Das ist eine Dauer / Zeitspanne.
Exercise 3
There have been problems with AIDS ________ many years.
📌 many years = wie viele Jahre? → Zeitspanne oder Zeitpunkt?
Exercise 4
South African people have achieved a lot ________ 1994.
📌 1994 = ein Jahr = genaues Datum / Startpunkt oder Dauer?
Exercise 5
Mia has lived in South Africa ________ she was young.
📌 Nach since kann auch ein ganzer Nebensatz folgen!
since + Subjekt + Verb = Startpunkt in der Vergangenheit
Exercise 6
I haven't visited the zoo ________ two years.
📌 two years = Wie lange schon? → Zeitspanne = for oder since?
Exercise 7
She has been a nurse ________ 6 o'clock this morning.
📌 6 o'clock = eine genaue Uhrzeit → Startpunkt oder Dauer?
Exercise 8
They have known each other ________ a long time.
📌 a long time beschreibt eine Zeitdauer, kein Datum und keinen Startpunkt.

Level C – Write full sentences. Form questions, negatives, or use for / since correctly. Hier kannst du die 🤖 KI-Hilfe besonders gut nutzen – sie analysiert genau, wo dein Fehler liegt!
Exercise 1 – Negative
Make it negative:  She / meet / a famous person / yet.
📌 Verneinungs-Formel: Subjekt + hasn't + Partizip + yet
Das Subjekt ist She → 3. Person Singular → hasn't (= has not)
Das Verb ist meet → unregelmäßig: meet – met – met
yet steht immer am Ende des Satzes bei Verneinungen.
✏️ Muster: She hasn't met a famous person yet.
Exercise 2 – Question
Write a question:  your friend / ever / be / on TV?
📌 Frage-Formel: Has + Subjekt + ever + Partizip?
Das Subjekt ist your friend = 3. Person Singular → Has am Anfang
Das Verb ist be → unregelmäßig: be – was – been
✏️ Muster: Has your friend ever been on TV?
Exercise 3 – for / since
Complete with for or since:  I / not see / Tom / last Monday.
📌 last Monday = ein genauer Zeitpunkt → since (nicht for!)
for = Zeitspanne (two years) · since = Zeitpunkt (last Monday, 1994)
Das Verb ist see → unregelmäßig: see – saw – seen
✏️ Muster: I haven't seen Tom since last Monday.
Exercise 4 – already
Use already:  The star / sing / in South Africa.
📌 Formel: Subjekt + has + already + Partizip
The star = he/she → 3. Person Singular → has
Das Verb ist sing → unregelmäßig: sing – sang – sung
✏️ Muster: The star has already sung in South Africa.
Exercise 5 – ever question
Write a question with ever:  you / listen / to South African music?
📌 Frage-Formel: Have + Subjekt + ever + Partizip?
Das Subjekt ist you → Have am Satzanfang (nicht Has!)
Das Verb ist listen → regelmäßig: listen + ed = listened
✏️ Muster: Have you ever listened to South African music?
