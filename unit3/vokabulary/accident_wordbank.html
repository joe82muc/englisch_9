📋 Word Bank – Useful Phrases

▼
👮 Police Officer
What is your name, address and phone number?
What is your date of birth?
What did you see?
What time did the accident happen?
Who was involved? How many cars / people were in the accident? What happened (next)?
Can you explain that in more detail, please?
Are you sure about that?
I see. / Really. / Interesting.
Thank you for your help.
We will get in touch if we have any more questions.
🧑 Witness
My name is …, my address is … and phone number …
My date of birth is …
First … / Then … / After that … / I think that …
I arrived at … / I think the accident happened … minutes before I arrived.
There was (a car) and (three) people in the accident.
The car / cyclist / van didn't stop / was going too fast.
Yes, I am. / I think so. / I'm not really sure now.
You're welcome. / No problem.
💡 Tipp: Use First … Then … After that … to structure your witness report in a logical order!

🎭 Write the dialogue – Roleplay
Exercise 1
👮 You are the police officer
The witness says: "My name is Lena Müller."
What do you ask next? (Ask for date of birth)
Exercise 2
🧑 You are the witness
Police asks: "What time did the accident happen?"
Answer using: I arrived at … / I think the accident happened … minutes before I arrived.
Exercise 3
🧑 You are the witness
Police asks: "What did you see?"
Write a short witness report (at least 2 sentences). Use: First … / Then … / After that …
Exercise 4
👮 You are the police officer
The witness gives a short answer. You want more information. Write a polite request for more detail.
Exercise 5 – Mini Dialogue 🎭
Situation: A cyclist had an accident near the school. You are a police officer. Write what you would say to end the interview politely after the witness has told you everything.
Write 2 sentences: thank the witness + say you will contact them if needed.
Exercise 6 – Your own roleplay ✍️
Situation: A car and a cyclist had an accident at 3:30 p.m. on Monday near the supermarket. There were 2 people involved. The van was going too fast.

Write a complete witness statement (5–6 sentences). Use phrases from the Word Bank.
